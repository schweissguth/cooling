3.5kW DX Split

  • Value Proposition
    • The Uniflair RM 3.5kW is a DX split cooling system.  In additional to all the Uniflair RM cooling benefits, the DX split keeps U-space consumption small by splitting it up into internal and external pieces – which means this system is designed to be room neutral i.e. the heat pulled from the rack is not rejected into the IT space.  This keeps the rack temperature consistent and stable.
  • Benefits
    • Cools virtually any rack up to 3.5kW
      • enough cooling to cool most single racks
      • no need for overprovisioned room based cooling (reduced cost, complexity, etc)
      • can also be used to introduce stability in environments with existing, unstable cooling
    • Only 5U height
      • fits discretely inside rack therefore no need to deploy external cooling
      • Higher cooling output in a smaller size because positioning is closest to the source; also greatly improves predictability
      • room neutral so therefore integration-friendly and closely positioned to IT equip for maximum predictability and smallest form factor >>can also serve as primary cooling in a room with
      • commercial cooling to prevent heat build up when building HVAC turns off for the weekend.
    • Bottom mounted
      • placed in bottom of rack to ensure maximum available and accessible U space
      • discretely placed to maximize integration potential
      • easily positioned into place and transportable with rack
    • Room-neutral (DX split)
      • the DX split system is designed to be room-neutral, which means the heat is fully rejected to the outdoors and therefore entirely self-sufficient (i.e. not self-contained) >>this does require
      • however mechanical piping between the indoor and outdoor pcs
    • Flexible for customer "self-contained“ configuration
      • to simplify installation, the condensing unit can be installed on top of the rack or adjacent to the rack so that the system essentially becomes self-contained. This way the system provides
      • point-cooling inside the rack while rejecting the heat outside of it. For this to work, the room in which the rack is placed must be large enough to absorb the heat or there must be sufficient
      • ventilation to manage it.

      • EMEA 50Hz
      • NAM 60Hz
